KHADII

Khadi is a hand-spun and hand-woven fabric that originated in India.
It is made from natural fibers like cotton, silk, or wool, and is known for its coarse texture and durability.
Khadi gained prominence during India's freedom movement, popularized by Mahatma Gandhi as a symbol of self-reliance and resistance against British rule.
Today, khadi represents sustainable and eco-friendly fashion, embodying traditional craftsmanship and cultural heritage.
